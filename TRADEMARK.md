OSMM's **code, schemas, and documentation are openly licensed** (see
[`LICENSING.md`](LICENSING.md)). The Marks are **not**. Open-source and
open-content licenses cover copyright; they do not grant trademark rights. This
policy explains how you may — and may not — use the Marks. Its purpose is the
same as the standard itself: so that when someone sees "OSMM," they can trust
what it refers to.

## What this policy protects

The value of an open standard depends on a reliable name. This policy keeps
"OSMM" meaning *the standard published in this repository*, so the name can't be
used to imply endorsement that wasn't given, or to brand a fork or product as
official when it isn't.

## Uses you may make without asking

- **Refer to the standard by name.** Use "OSMM" / "Open Semantic Marketing
  Model" in articles, docs, talks, and posts to refer to this standard. Truthful
  reference (nominative use) is always fine.
- **State compatibility, truthfully.** If your tool reads or writes OSMM objects
  that validate against the published schemas, you may say it is
  **"OSMM-compatible,"** **"built on OSMM,"** or **"supports OSMM."** Describe
  *your* product's relationship to OSMM; don't imply OSMM/DoW produces or
  endorses your product.
- **Link to the project** using an unmodified logo from [`brand/`](brand/),
  following [`brand/LOGO.md`](brand/LOGO.md).

## Uses that require written permission

- Using the Marks in **your** company name, product name, domain name, app name,
  social handle, or logo.
- Using the Marks in a way that suggests OSMM or DoW **sponsors, endorses, or is
  affiliated with** your product, service, or organization.
- Using the Marks on merchandise, or as the primary branding of an event,
  publication, or community.
- Using the Marks for a **modified or forked** version of OSMM in a way that
  could be mistaken for the official standard. You may state that your fork *is
  derived from* OSMM; you may not present it *as* OSMM.

Requests: open an issue in this repository describing your intended use. A
trademark or licensing request is a normal, non-confidential inquiry, so a public
issue is fine.

## Conformance claims

"OSMM-compatible" is an honest, self-asserted statement that your implementation
conforms to the published schemas in [`schemas/`](schemas). It is **not** a
certification. DoW does not currently run a certification or compliance program,
and no use of the Marks may state or imply that DoW has certified, audited, or
approved your implementation. If a formal certification mark or program is
established later, this section will be updated.

## Logo use

Use the logo files in [`brand/`](brand/) **unmodified**, and follow the clear-
space, minimum-size, color, and do/don't rules in [`brand/LOGO.md`](brand/LOGO.md).
Don't recolor (beyond the supplied reversed/mono files), distort, re-letter, or
add effects to the Marks, and don't combine them with your own marks into a
single lockup.
